Welcome to the Official
Website of Dr. Adetayo Sotayo

I am Dr Adeayo Sotayo (Ade). I grew up in Lagos, Nigeria and attended Chrisland College (secondary) and Oxbridge Tutorial College (foundation year). At 17, I moved to the UK, where I started a degree in Mechanical Engineering and finished with a First Class in 2013, and emerged as the top student in my class. I went on to study for a PhD in Engineering at Lancaster University and was given an exemption from the Master’s degree. Thereafter, I completed my PhD thesis in three years at the age of 23! Some of my academic honours and achievements are available here. Following my interest in personal development, I wrote a blog discussing my university experience, and a year later, won the ‘Blogger of the Year’ award in a university-wide competition.

I have worked on a diverse range of projects, which have been published in peer-reviewed journals and conference proceedings. My research interests include Engineering Materials and Design, Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing), Industry 4.0, Timber and Composite Structures, Recycling, Sustainability and Waste management. I previously worked as a Researcher/Lecturer at the University of Liverpool and currently work as a Research Fellow/Lecturer in Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing) working towards Industry 4.0. This is part of a European Union project entitled SAM (Sector Skills Strategy in Additive Manufacturing) at Brunel University London.

I am also a Writer and Speaker, and I have presented around the world, including in Sweden, South Korea, Italy, Belgium, France, Germany, Ireland, Portugal and the UK. I have supervised several Undergraduates, Masters and PhD Students, worked on technical R&D projects, supported businesses and SMEs, and also keen to be part of the development of innovative solutions to overcome technical challenges, therefore, I am interested in new collaborations. Please get in touch.
